Suresh Raina: Indian Cricket Star

Biography

Suresh Raina is a former Indian cricketer who played as a left-handed middle-order batsman and occasional off-spin bowler. He is known for his aggressive batting and leadership qualities on the field. Raina is also one of the few cricketers to have represented India in all three formats: Test, One Day International (ODI), and Twenty20 (T20) cricket.

Born on November 27, 1986, in Muradnagar, Uttar Pradesh, Raina made his debut for India in 2005 and quickly became a key player for the national team. He was a crucial member of the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup-winning team and is also known for his success in the Indian Premier League (IPL), where he captained the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) and led them to multiple titles.

Early Career

Suresh Raina's journey to cricket stardom began at a young age. He was discovered while playing for Uttar Pradesh in domestic cricket, and his powerful performances earned him a place in the national team. Raina's breakthrough came when he was selected for the 2005 India U-19 team that toured England, and soon after, he was given an opportunity in the senior team.

Achievements
